Indirect Dependency Metatheorem

Metatheorem

Let \( C \) be a conjecture in a formal system. If \( C \) implies a false statement \( S \), where \( S \) is not part of the core logical structure of \( C \) (i.e., \( S \) is derived but not intrinsic to the primary reasoning of \( C \)), then there exists a point \( P \) such that:

\[ C \Rightarrow S \quad \text{and} \quad S \notin \text{Core}(C) \quad \Rightarrow \quad \exists P \, \text{s.t.} \, C \Rightarrow S \, \text{at} \, P \quad \Rightarrow \quad C \equiv \text{False}. \]

Formal Proof

To prove the statement, we follow these logical steps:

  1. Base Assumptions: We begin by assuming that \( C \) is a conjecture in some formal system and that \( C \) implies a false statement \( S \). This is our starting point: \[ C \Rightarrow S \quad \text{where} \quad S \text{ is false}. \]
  2. Core Structure Definition: Since \( S \) is not part of the core logical structure of \( C \), we know that: \[ S \notin \text{Core}(C) \]
  3. Existence of Point \( P \): We assert that there exists a point \( P \) in the logical reasoning process of \( C \), where \( C \) depends on \( S \). Even though \( S \) is not part of the core structure, it may be introduced as part of an intermediary step or conditional argument. This point \( P \) can be thought of as a juncture in the reasoning where \( S \) influences the outcome of \( C \).
  4. Contradiction: Since \( S \) is false, we conclude that the presence of \( S \) forces \( C \) to be false: \[ C \equiv \text{False}. \]

Therefore, we have proven that if \( C \) implies a false statement \( S \) and \( S \) is not part of the core reasoning of \( C \), then \( C \) is false, and there exists a point \( P \) where the dependency on \( S \) arises.
